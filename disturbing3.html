CHILD LABOUR
In the world's poorest countries, around 25% of children are engaged in child labour. In Mali, Benin, Chad and Guinea-Bissau, there are over 50% of children aged 5-14 working. Children are predominantly employed by their parents, rather than factories, and work predominetly in agriculture.

There are estimated 168 million children worldwide involved in child labour.

CHILDREN IN COLONIES
Systematic use of child labour was common place in the colonies of European powers between 1650 and 1950. Sometimes they were used to punish disobedient slaves. When a worker didn't gather the required amount of cotton, the hand of his son or daughter might have been cut off as punishment.
